# The Rescue Playbook

**A Repeatable Framework for Turning Churn Risk into Renewal**

*Professional Services × Customer Success*

---

## Framework Overview

Every customer rescue follows the same arc — six phases that move an account from crisis to renewal. This playbook codifies that process into a repeatable, scalable methodology.

| Phase | Name | Objective |
|-------|------|-----------|
| 01 | **Triage** | Assess the full scope of the crisis before acting |
| 02 | **Discover** | Listen deep and diagnose the real root causes |
| 03 | **Plan** | Chart a transparent recovery roadmap |
| 04 | **Deliver** | Create undeniable, hands-on value |
| 05 | **Elevate** | Align executives on ROI and the path forward |
| 06 | **Bridge** | Transition from recovery into renewal |

---

## Phase 1: Triage — Assess the Damage

**Objective:** Understand the full scope of the crisis before taking any action.

### Activities

- **Review account history:** Contracts, past touchpoints, escalation timeline, previous interventions. Understand what was tried and why it failed.
- **Assess four dimensions:**
  - *Trust:* How does the customer feel about the partnership?
  - *Adoption:* How deeply are they using the product?
  - *Issues:* What open complaints/tickets exist?
  - *Stakeholders:* Who are the key players and what are their stances?
- **Classify the root cause:** Product problem, process problem, or people problem? Most churn risks are not product failures — they are breakdowns in enablement, communication, or internal customer processes.
- **Identify your Point of Contact (POC):** The person on the customer side who will be your partner in recovery. Must be invested in success and willing to collaborate closely.

> **Key Insight:** Don't diagnose until you've listened. Don't prescribe until you've diagnosed.

**Output:** A clear-eyed assessment of account health, a root cause hypothesis, and an identified POC.

---

## Phase 2: Discover — Listen Deep, Diagnose Root Causes

**Objective:** Build trust through genuine listening while surfacing the real problems behind stated complaints.

### Activities

- **Start with the POC.** Let them tell the full story — frustrations, team pain, what's been tried. Listen without defending.
- **Interview every impacted team.** Ask: What are your real complaints? What would you want to achieve? What business questions are you trying to answer?
- **Perform a technical/data audit.** Separate platform issues from usage issues. Audit implementation, data taxonomy, and configuration. Often, the problem is how the tool was set up — not the tool itself.
- **Map stated complaints vs. actual root causes.** Many complaints are symptoms, not causes. A user saying "this feature doesn't work" may reveal a knowledge gap. Surface these with empathy, then demonstrate what's possible.
- **Identify organisational silos.** Look for teams working in isolation who share similar goals. Siloed processes are one of the biggest barriers to value realisation.
- **Reframe business questions.** Assess whether questions are *reporting-oriented* ("How many subscribers in France?") or *decision-oriented* ("What behaviours predict subscriber churn in France, and what's our target retention rate?"). The former produces dashboards. The latter produces outcomes.

> **Key Technique:** Challenge with empathy. When a complaint stems from a knowledge gap, don't say "you're wrong" — demonstrate the capability. Let the revelation come from their own experience.

**Output:** Root cause analysis, stakeholder map, knowledge gap inventory, and a clear picture of what needs to change.

---

## Phase 3: Plan — Chart the Recovery

**Objective:** Define a structured, transparent recovery roadmap the customer believes in because they helped shape it.

### Activities

- **Create the action plan** with milestones, owners, and timelines. Share openly with the customer.
- **Heal before you build.** No upsells. No new feature pitches. No expansion conversations. Clean the wound first. Sales motions at this stage destroy trust.
- **Align internal resources.** Coordinate Value Engineering, Training, Product, Executive Sponsors. Define roles clearly. If internal ownership is lacking, be prepared to carry the weight.
- **Establish a cadence.** Regular check-ins with the POC. Consistent presence signals commitment; unreliable follow-up signals deprioritisation.

> **Key Insight:** The plan exists to rebuild trust as much as to organise work. A customer who helped define the roadmap and sees you executing on it starts to believe the partnership can work.

**Output:** A recovery action plan with milestones, clear ownership, established cadence, and internal alignment.

---

## Phase 4: Deliver — Create Undeniable Value

**Objective:** Prove the platform's value through tangible, tailored outcomes. This is where the rescue is won or lost.

### Activities

- **Build tailored solutions.** Custom dashboards and analyses for each team's specific business questions. Don't just advise — build. Show, don't tell.
- **Coach the POC.** Intensive 1:1 technical coaching to build internal expertise. The POC becomes the champion who sustains momentum after you step back.
- **Run cross-team workshops.** Bring siloed teams together around shared objectives. Joint workshops create alignment, reduce duplication, and demonstrate value across the organisation.
- **Be proactively generous.** Anticipate needs and deliver before being asked. Go further than expected — open your own product account, create demo environments, build proof-of-concept workflows.
- **Use competitive benchmarking.** "Your competitors use the same platform and are successful. The gap isn't the product — it's process." This empowers customers to close their own gaps.
- **Deliver on-site when possible.** Physical presence compounds trust. The investment signals commitment at a level remote engagement cannot match.

> **Core Principle:** Create strategic dependency through value. By doing the work, you become indispensable. The customer's experience shifts from scepticism to reliance. This is the turning point of every rescue.

**Output:** Tangible deliverables per team, an upskilled POC, cross-team alignment, and a customer who has *experienced* value — not been told about it.

---

## Phase 5: Elevate — Executive Alignment & ROI

**Objective:** Lock in the value story at the executive level with quantified business impact.

### Activities

- **Executive Business Review.** Not a product demo — a strategic alignment session. Bring your executives to signal this customer matters at the highest level.
- **Build ROI frameworks.** Connect platform usage to business outcomes with concrete numbers. Develop measurement frameworks (e.g., quantifying revenue impact of technical errors).
- **Let the customer speak.** By now, the customer should be voicing the transformation themselves. Their advocacy is earned, not manufactured.
- **Align on the future.** The EBR is not just a look-back. What does the next chapter look like? This sets the stage for renewal.

> **Key Insight:** Reusable assets created during this phase have value beyond a single account. Document them. Share them across the organisation.

**Output:** Executive-level validation, quantified ROI story, forward-looking strategy, and reusable frameworks for the broader org.

---

## Phase 6: Bridge — From Recovery to Renewal

**Objective:** Transition the rescued account into a healthy, sustainable renewal conversation.

### Activities

- **Validate sentiment shift** across multiple stakeholders — not just the POC. A rescue isn't complete until the organisation's perception has shifted.
- **Create success plans** for each team and use case. These ensure continuity regardless of account ownership changes.
- **Warm handoff to Sales** with full context: what was broken, what was fixed, the ROI story, and current sentiment.
- **Don't disappear.** Maintain personal connection through the renewal period. A sudden absence after months of intense engagement feels like abandonment.

> **Key Insight:** The rescue is not complete at renewal. It's complete when the account is self-sustaining — when the customer has internal champions, working processes, and confidence to extract value independently.

**Output:** A customer entering renewal from a position of trust. Documented success plans. A Sales team armed with context.

## 10 Core Principles

| # | Principle | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 01 | **Extreme Ownership** | When no one else steps up, you step up. Own the outcome entirely. |
| 02 | **Listen Before You Act** | Deep listening builds trust and reveals root causes. First meetings are for absorbing. |
| 03 | **Heal Before You Build** | Address existing pain before proposing anything new. Clean the wound first. |
| 04 | **Do the Work** | Don't just advise. Build dashboards, run analyses, create demos. Tangible > theoretical. |
| 05 | **Challenge With Respect** | Validate feelings, challenge assumptions. Knowledge gaps are your biggest opportunity. |
| 06 | **Break the Silos** | Connect teams with shared goals who don't know they share them. |
| 07 | **Reframe the Questions** | Shift from "what are the numbers?" to "what decisions will you make?" |
| 08 | **Be Proactively Generous** | Anticipate needs and deliver before being asked. Exceed expectations consistently. |
| 09 | **Create Strategic Dependency** | Make yourself indispensable through quality delivery. Scepticism → reliance → renewal. |
| 10 | **Use Competitive Pressure Wisely** | Benchmark against peers to motivate, not intimidate. Empower the customer to close their own gaps. |

## Case Study: Canal+

**Industry:** Global Media & Entertainment
**Risk Level:** Active churn — customer considering non-renewal
**Outcome:** Moved from churn risk to renewal negotiations

### Starting State

- Zero trust in the platform across the organisation
- Product perceived as "only good for reading numbers, not taking action"
- Minimal adoption limited to basic reporting
- Director of Data planning to remove key use cases from taxonomy
- Multiple teams with open complaints and unresolved issues
- Siloed teams with no cross-functional collaboration
- Relationship at its lowest point

### Actions Taken

1. Conducted comprehensive data and taxonomy audit — confirmed the problem was implementation and process, not platform capability
2. Interviewed every impacted team individually — surfaced real needs, knowledge gaps, and siloed processes
3. Reframed business questions from reporting-oriented to decision-oriented — helped teams define target metrics and actionable KPIs
4. Built tailored dashboards and analyses for each team's specific business questions
5. Delivered intensive 1:1 technical coaching with the customer's POC
6. Created proof-of-concept demonstrations using real product data
7. Connected siloed teams around shared objectives — created cross-functional alignment
8. Delivered 17 on-site workshops in Paris
9. Developed a reusable framework for measuring revenue impact of technical errors (later adopted by other accounts)
10. Coordinated Executive Business Review with C-suite leadership

### Results

- Complete sentiment transformation across all stakeholder levels
- Customer publicly praised the turnaround of the partnership
- Platform recognised as a strategic decision-making tool
- Increased adoption with tailored dashboards per team
- Account moved from active churn risk to renewal negotiations
- Revenue impact framework became a reusable asset for other accounts

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Risk | Instead |
|-------------|------|---------|
| **Pitching During Pain** | Sales pitches on a frustrated customer accelerate churn | Acknowledge pain. Fix what's broken. Discuss expansion only after trust is restored. |
| **Surface-Level Check-Ins** | Status calls without substance signal you have nothing to offer | Show up with deliverables. Every interaction should demonstrate progress. |
| **Ownership Diffusion** | Multiple people "involved" but no one accountable | One person owns the rescue. Others support. One throat to choke, one hand to shake. |
| **Advising Without Doing** | Strategy without execution is a slide deck gathering dust | Build the dashboard. Create the analysis. Do the work, then walk them through it. |
| **Ignoring Internal Silos** | Treating teams in isolation misses compounding value | Map shared goals. Create joint workshops. Let teams discover common ground. |
| **Vanishing After the Win** | Disengaging before renewal closes destroys rebuilt trust | Stay present through renewal. The Bridge phase matters as much as the rescue. |
